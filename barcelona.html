Get to Know An Interview

Interviews might sound intimidating. Don’t worry! We have a lot of resources, from interview preparation, practice, and execution.


These resources could help you quickly build understanding and a skillset of interview, even if you have little or no experience before. If you are an experienced interviewee already, that’s great! We also provide you with some tips and mock interview sessions to build your confidence and skills even further.

First, we should understand what the interview is.


An interview is a 30-45 minute pitch of you. It is the time for you to demonstrate you can do the job, to show the employer the value add you’d bring to the company! It is also an opportunity for you to assess whether or not you’d want to work at the organization/with these people/in this role.

Behavioral Interviews

Answering Behavioral Interview Questions

The question, “Tell me about yourself” might be asked to you multiple times throughout an interview process. When you meet with different people in the organization, they will want to know your story! The best way to feel confident about answering this question is to prepare for a passionate, concise answer.


Follow these guidelines to craft your answer to this question:

  1. Provide a brief overview of past work and educational experiences that led you to where you are now
  2. Convey story of passion for the field/industry/org!
  3. Keep it Relevant- Provide a few relevant points that directly tie to the position and how you are qualified for it
  4. Specific past work experience, specific skills and strengths that would be valuable to the position/organization
  5. Keep it Concise- No more than 1 to 2 minutes!

Here ares some examples you can reference when crafting your answer


BSI Example

I am a [junior/senior] at the University of Michigan studying [area of study] through the School of Information. Our school focuses on using technology to build a better world, and I am passionate about [specific subject/interest]. Throughout my time at U-M, I have [name a few examples of projects/clubs/past experiences that have added to your passion/experience]. I was so excited to see that this position was open and am looking forward to learning more about it today.


MSI Example

I am earning a [degree title] from the University of Michigan School of Information specializing in [area]. I am passionate about [specific area/interest] and have [name one or two projects you’ve worked on in that area]. I was excited to see that you had positions open because [Explain why you are passionate about their company/specific position and mention one or two related projects/accomplishments].
